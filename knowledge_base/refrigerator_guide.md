# Refrigerator Troubleshooting Guide

## Problem: Refrigerator Not Cooling

### Possible Causes

* Dirty condenser coils
* Faulty thermostat
* Blocked air vents
* Compressor malfunction
* Damaged door gasket

### Troubleshooting Steps

1. Verify that the refrigerator is receiving power.
2. Check the thermostat setting.
3. Clean condenser coils using a brush or vacuum.
4. Ensure air vents inside the refrigerator are not blocked.
5. Inspect door seals for damage.

### Recommended Solution

Clean condenser coils and adjust thermostat settings. If cooling does not improve, inspect the compressor and thermostat.

### Safety Precautions

* Disconnect power before cleaning coils.
* Avoid touching electrical components with wet hands.

## Problem: Water Leakage

### Possible Causes

* Clogged defrost drain
* Cracked water supply line
* Overflowing drain pan

### Troubleshooting Steps

1. Locate the defrost drain.
2. Remove debris blocking the drain.
3. Inspect water lines for cracks.
4. Check the drain pan for overflow.

### Recommended Solution

Clear the drain and replace damaged water lines.

### Safety Precautions

* Disconnect power before inspecting internal components.

---

## Problem: Excessive Frost Build-Up

### Possible Causes

* Door left open
* Faulty door gasket
* Defrost system failure

### Troubleshooting Steps

1. Inspect door seal integrity.
2. Ensure the door closes completely.
3. Check the automatic defrost system.

### Recommended Solution

Replace damaged door seals and repair the defrost system if necessary.
